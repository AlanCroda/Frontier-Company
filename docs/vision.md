# Vision

## Project identity

Frontier Company is a compact, systems-driven strategy game built in Unity by a solo developer. Its approved time model is Real-Time with Pause (RTWP), operating on a simultaneous tick-based system.

The player builds and controls a small organization operating inside a changing regional ecosystem. The game should create strategic pressure through systemic interaction, contextual risk, and the consequences of player commitments rather than through a large content volume.

## Design pillars

- **Ownership:** the player should feel responsible for the organization they build and the consequences it creates.
- **Company identity:** the organization should develop a recognizable character through player choices and constraints.
- **Meaningful risk:** choices should involve tradeoffs, exposure, and consequences rather than guaranteed optimization.
- **Systemic interaction:** important outcomes should emerge from interacting systems, not isolated one-off mechanics.
- **Contextual strategy:** good decisions should depend on the current regional situation and company state.
- **Emergent consequences:** the game should support surprising but understandable outcomes.
- **Bounded campaigns:** play should have finishable campaign arcs rather than endless sprawl.
- **Finishable scope:** the project should stay compact enough for solo development.
- **Early testability:** core assumptions should become playable and testable early.

## Scope principles

- Prefer depth from interaction over breadth from content volume.
- Prefer compact systems that can be observed, tuned, and explained.
- Prefer playable validation chunks over large speculative design documents.
- Keep campaign scope bounded.
- Make consequences readable enough that the player can learn from them.
- Avoid committing to detailed mechanics before they are needed for a playable chunk.

## Non-goals

- Frontier Company is not intended to be an endless grand strategy game.
- Frontier Company is not intended to be a maximalist tycoon simulation.
- Frontier Company is not intended to rely on a large authored content library.
- Frontier Company is not multiplayer-first.
- Frontier Company should not require large-team production processes.

## Deliberately undecided

The following areas are intentionally not specified yet:

- exact economy model;
- exact regional simulation model;
- company roles, jobs, units, or staff structure;
- resources, markets, or production chains;
- map structure;
- campaign structure;
- tick length, timing values, and simulation scheduling details;
- user interface layout;
- balance values;
- testing framework.
